About the Project

Touch metal. Play music.

The Capacitive Touch Piano is a low-power embedded instrument built on a custom PCB. Touch any of eight copper pads and the board responds immediately: a corresponding LED lights up and a piezo buzzer sounds the note. The system also supports octave shifting across three modes and a record-and-replay function for saving and playing back short melodies.

The computational brain is a Texas Instruments MSPM0G3507 microcontroller, which communicates with an NXP MPR121 capacitive touch sensor controller over a software-implemented I²C bus. The MPR121 monitors each pad's capacitance continuously; a touch is registered when the reading crosses a programmable threshold above baseline. The MSPM0 reads that event and drives the LED and buzzer output in real time.
